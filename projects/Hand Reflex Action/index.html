HAND REFLEX ACTION

Reflex actions are quick, automatic, and protective responses to stimuli that occur without conscious control. One of the most common examples is the hand withdrawal reflex — when we instantly pull our hand away after touching something hot or sharp. This process involves a specialized neural pathway known as a reflex arc. In this project, we recreate this life-saving mechanism using an Arduino-based setup to demonstrate how the body reacts within milliseconds to protect itself from potential harm.

The reflex arc consists of three main components — a sensory neuron, the spinal cord, and a motor neuron. When a stimulus is detected, sensory signals travel to the spinal cord, which processes the input and sends an immediate response signal to the motor neuron. This bypasses the brain to ensure a rapid reaction. In our simulation, this concept is replicated using electronic components that mimic these biological functions.

The working of our model is simple yet powerful:

• The LM35 sensor detects rising temperature, acting as the sensory receptor.
• The Arduino processes this data — functioning as the spinal cord.
• When the temperature crosses a predefined threshold, the servo motor is triggered.
• The servo moves the mechanical hand model away, symbolizing the body’s protective response.

This precise simulation highlights the beauty of biological reflexes — a perfect blend of speed and safety without requiring conscious thought.

This project successfully replicates the concept of a hand reflex action using an Arduino, LM35 temperature sensor, and a servo motor. It offers a tangible understanding of how sensory input and motor response are linked through neural coordination. Beyond biology, this setup bridges the gap between technology and physiology — showing how electronic systems can mimic natural human responses in an educational and visually engaging way.
